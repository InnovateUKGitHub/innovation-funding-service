Background

  1. These terms and conditions apply to organisations who are applying for grant funding under the UK Government’s Underwrite Guarantee Programme and Extended Underwrite Guarantee Programme for Horizon 2020 (the ‘Programme’), which is managed by UK Research and Innovation (‘we’,‘us’, ‘our’ or ‘UKRI’). Each organisation must accept the terms and conditions before submitting its application.
  2. If you are successful, you will receive a grant offer letter confirming the conditions of the award. This letter must be countersigned and uploaded by your organisation according to our instructions before your project can start. In submitting your application, you accept that we intend to replace these general terms and conditions with others tailored to the Programme. If the grant offer letter sent to you contains, attaches or refers to alternative terms and conditions, they will replace these terms and conditions. By countersigning and uploading the letter you will be accepting the replacement terms and conditions sent with it.
  3. If you do not countersign and upload the letter, we may immediately terminate these terms and conditions and require you to repay some or all grant payments made to you by us.

1. General terms and conditions

We reserve the right to change or replace these grant terms and conditions unilaterally and at any time upon notice. You cannot assign, transfer or sub-contract any of your rights or obligations under this agreement to any third party.

Rights and/or remedies under this award, whether exercised or not, remain available throughout the period that these terms and conditions apply.

An award does not create any partnership or joint venture in law.

We:

  • accept no liability for any consequences, whether direct or indirect, that may come about by undertaking your project, using the grant or ending the award
  • limit our liability to the amount of eligible award for which you can provide evidence

Our awards are subject to the laws of England and Wales.

An award cannot be used for any political or lobbying activity or purpose.

2. Duration

These terms and conditions come into effect from the start date until the end date provided in the grant offer letter, or until the award is terminated.

3. Your obligations

As a successful applicant, you will:

  • make sure that your project is managed in accordance with the terms and conditions of your Horizon 2020 agreement with an EU institution (‘H2020 Agreement’) and/or (if one exists) any agreement with the lead partner or co-ordinator (‘Consortium Agreement’) and these terms and conditions
  • make sure that you do not terminate the H2020 Agreement or the Consortium Agreement
  • provide all information requested by us to enable us to assess your organisation’s eligibility for grant funding
  • inform us promptly of any issue or material change that may affect the progress, delivery or exploitation of your project
  • inform us promptly if the H2020 Agreement or Consortium Agreement has been terminated
  • inform us promptly if you receive any payments from the EU institution, lead partner/co-ordinator
  • within 3 months of the end of your project, you will provide us with the final project report and confirmation that the final claim and final Independent Accountant’s Report have been submitted

The award is subject to your project starting within 90 days from our email notification to you. The award is also subject to us receiving confirmation from HM Treasury that payments can be made under the Programme. We reserve the right to withdraw the offer of the award if the partners do not complete their project and bank details within 30 days of notification. This information is needed so that we can start our pre-project checks.

4. Payment of grant

You accept that:

  • we will only make payments to you in accordance with these terms and conditions on a benevolent basis to assist you to continue to deliver your project; and
  • the payments do not remedy or discharge any losses that you may have suffered as a result of the actions of third parties (including but not limited to the EU institutions).

We will pay your grant by instalments quarterly and in arrears on submission of a claim, unless otherwise agreed in your grant offer letter. This will be against your net eligible costs incurred and paid over the duration of your project. Payments will be made in pounds sterling (£) in accordance with the Foreign Exchange Methodology published by us. This methodology may be amended from time to time, setting out how any outstanding Horizon 2020 grant payments represented in euros (€) will be converted into pound sterling (£).

Your claims must be supported by all of the following:

  • evidence that either:
    • the EU institution, lead partner or co-ordinator has rejected your application for payment
    • the EU institution, lead partner or co-ordinator has failed to pay when due
    • or the EU institution, lead partner or co-ordinator has clawed back payments made under the H2020 Agreement or Consortium Agreement (in each case otherwise than due to a breach of the H2020 Agreement or Consortium Agreement)
  • a revised forecast of eligible project costs which details expected costs for each future claim period and highlights any variations from the last forecast
  • an independent accountant’s report (see paragraph 7)

Claims must be for completed quarters only, except for the final quarter where you may submit a claim for costs incurred but not necessarily paid, provided that the cost is:

  • for labour, overheads and/or travel and subsistence only
  • paid within 30 days of the end of your project
  • included within the final independent accountant’s report

We will normally pay the grant within 30 days, unless we need further information to support the claim. We agree to do this within 14 days of receiving the claim.

We do not have to pay any claim received more than 30 days after the end of the quarter to which it relates. We will not pay any claims received later than 3 months after completion of your project.

We reserve the right not to consider new applications from organisations who have failed to return required financial reports on any projects funded by UKRI.

We will not pay more than 85% of the grant until all outstanding claims and supporting information have been received and the final independent accountant’s report has been approved.

You are responsible for maintaining detailed records and documentation relating to your award. These should provide evidence that your eligible project costs comply with all State aid rules, as outlined in paragraph 9. These records must be kept for 10 years following the award and if requested must be supplied to the Commission within 20 days.

We may appoint an auditor to ensure you are complying with the terms and conditions. You agree to give the auditor access to your project records within 2 weeks of notice of their appointment. If the auditor determines that you should repay the grant to us, we may recover the cost of the auditor’s work from you.

5. Monitoring

Your project will be monitored in accordance with our project monitoring framework. You will meet with your assigned monitoring officer as often as is specified at the start of your project. You will allow them access to your employees, agents, premises, facilities and records to review your progress, and send them any information, explanations and documents requested within 10 days.

6. Suspension, withdrawal and repayment of grant

We may suspend, withdraw or reclaim the grant in whole or in part.

We will take appropriate care in how we do this, but in certain circumstances we can immediately suspend grant payments.

If we have concerns that mean we might need to suspend, withdraw or reclaim the grant we will write to inform you and you will then have 30 days in which to address our concerns.

Examples of events that may result in us suspending grant payments include:

  • misuse of the grant funds, including in a fraudulent or financially misleading way (or for purposes not related to your project)
  • false statements in any part of your application or project
  • failure to maintain satisfactory progress on your project
  • any material changes to the proposed outcomes of your project
  • failure to comply with all terms and conditions

Examples of events that may result in us stopping grant payments permanently include:

  • failure to resolve to our satisfaction, or not being able to resolve, the reasons for suspension
  • being found to have applied for and secured multiple awards for the same or largely interchangeable project scopes
  • being found to have breached any of our policies
  • becoming insolvent, declared bankrupt, placed into receivership, administration or liquidation, or having a petition presented for winding up, or any similar arrangements that may affect you financially (including any requirements imposed by GBER (see paragraph 9)
  • the H2020 Agreement or Consortium Agreement terminates
  • the EU institution, lead partner or co-ordinator agrees to make the grant payments
  • HM Treasury requires us to suspend, withdraw or terminate payments under the Programme
  • we have reasonable grounds to believe that the grant payments to you breach State aid law
  • a change to your legal status which you have failed to declare or have misrepresented to us

If you do not resolve an issue causing suspension or if we stop the grant payments, we may require you to repay some or all of the grant.

If at any time we notify you that we intend to change or replace any or all of these terms and conditions and you do not accept the change or replacement, we may immediately terminate the grant and require you to repay some or all grant payments made to you by us.

7. Financial reporting

You agree to appoint an independent accountant who will inspect your financial statements relating to the grant. They will prepare an independent accountant’s report (IAR) using our standard template, at the intervals stated in the relevant guidance note. The report will contain an opinion that:

  • the financial information and level of grant claimed are accurate and legitimate
  • eligible costs that you have submitted have been properly incurred and paid
  • you have provided satisfactory explanations for any queries raised by the independent accountant

You must appoint an independent accountant in a manner consistent with our Standard Terms of Engagement of Accountants. We will send these to you.

In some circumstances, you may be asked to provide your latest published annual accounts or management accounts, that do not breach any law, order or regulation restricting such disclosure.

We may also require you to submit an accountant’s report if any of the following occur:

  • you withdraw from the project
  • your project is terminated
  • you submit a claim disclosing expenditure substantially greater than forecast
  • you are suspected of breaching the terms and conditions

8. Confidentiality and information management

We have the right to request access to any additional information we feel necessary in connection with this award. You must provide or allow access to such information within 2 weeks of a request. We will treat your information in accordance with our policies and personal information charter.

9. State aid obligations

You must ensure that the grant funding complies with State aid law.

We will stop grant payments if you become subject to a recovery order that follows on from a previous Commission decision, which declares any aid you have received illegal and incompatible with the internal market.

If any grant is found to be unlawful State aid you may be required by an order of the European Commission or the Competition and Markets Authority to repay it to us. Interest will be charged on the amount being reclaimed from the date of payment at the applicable legislated rate.

No subcontract may be made which would constitute a breach of State aid obligations.

10. Intellectual property

Any intellectual property rights (IPR) developed during or as a result of your project are owned by you.

You will ensure that you have the necessary rights to use or access third party IPR needed to carry out your project.

11. Exploitation of project results

We expect you to refine and update your exploitation plan over your project’s lifetime.

For a period of 5 years from the end of your project, we expect you to take reasonable steps to exploit the results of your project in the UK or wider European Economic Area. We may evaluate the impact of funding awarded to your project. We expect you to assist us in this.

12. UK statutory framework

We are required to comply with all legislation in England and Wales. We particularly draw to your attention the data protection legislation, the Bribery Act 2010, the Fraud Act 2006 and the Modern Slavery Act 2015.

We expect you to comply with all legislation (including in the devolved administrations) where they apply to you and to act in a way that does not affect our ability to comply.

13. Publication of information

We encourage you to seek your own publicity. If you want to publicise your project in any way, please read our guide ‘Publicising your project: guidelines from UKRI’ which you can get from UKRI’s press office by emailing press@ukri.org.

14. Dispute resolution

If a dispute arises between you and UKRI, we advise your chief executive officer contacts us to discuss, in good faith, how the matter can be resolved. If a mutually acceptable resolution cannot be found, the parties agree to refer the matter to the London Court of International Arbitration (LCIA), whose decision will be binding.

15. Force majeure

Where an event of force majeure, or a change to the Laws of England and Wales, UK government or devolved administration policy and/or the Legal Acts of the European Union occurs which affects our ability to continue funding your project, we may terminate the award by giving you as much notice as possible in writing. In such circumstances, we will meet any eligible costs you have reasonably incurred and paid prior to the date of termination.

By submitting an application, you are accepting these terms and conditions and any change and/or replacement to these terms and conditions notified to you in accordance with paragraph 1.
