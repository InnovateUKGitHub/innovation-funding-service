Innovate UK will pay your grant by instalments quarterly and in arrears on submission of a claim, unless otherwise agreed in your grant offer letter. This will be against your net eligible costs incurred and paid over the duration of the project.

Your claims must be supported by:

Innovate UK does not have to pay any claim received more than 30 days after the end of the quarter to which it relates. We will not pay any claims received later than 3 months after completion of the project.

Innovate UK reserves the right not to consider new applications from organisations who have failed to return the required financial reports on any projects funded by Innovate UK.

Innovate UK will not pay more than 85% of the grant until all outstanding claims and supporting information have been received and the final independent accountant's report has been approved.

You are responsible for maintaining detailed records and documentation relating to your award. These should provide evidence that your eligible project costs comply with all State aid rules, as outlined in paragraph 9. Schedule 1
Your relationship with the Advanced Propulsion Centre UK

This schedule provides an overview of the relationship and requirements between you and the Advanced Propulsion Centre (APC) UK on the award of any grant.

1. Support from APC and payments to APC

APC will work with you to give support:

  • during the application, project monitoring and close out phases
  • with industry engagement events for the project team
  • in reporting overall progress to the Automotive Council UK and BEIS

APC will be remunerated for the services it provides, as outlined in paragraph 19 of the Innovate UK terms and conditions of an Advanced Propulsion Centre UK competition grant award. Academic partners are not exempt from this requirement.

This fee will be invoiced by APC, on a quarterly basis, to each project partner in line with submission of claims to Innovate UK. Unless otherwise agreed between APC and a project partner, project partners are expected to pay the fee within 30 days of the invoice date.

For the avoidance of doubt, APC is VAT registered in the United Kingdom, and will charge VAT, where applicable, at the prevailing rate.

2. Core Projects Publication Request and Asset Release Form

Under the terms of the agreement between you and the APC, the lead applicant shall provide the APC with a copy of the Core Projects Publication Request and Asset Release Form. This will be supplied to the lead applicant as part of the project set-up phase. The return of the form is an absolute condition of the award of your grant.

Any information provided by a project partner in this form, including any commercially sensitive information, will become publicly accessible.

3. Annual project review

Your project will be subject to an annual review. You will be required to present an updated strategic rationale for the project to the APC.

The purpose of the review is to understand how changes in business environments have altered:

  • the strategic alignment of the project partners with the outputs of the project
  • the commercial viability of the technologies and other outputs developed through the project
  • the commitment of the project partners to finance the next stages of the project and the ensuing commercialisation of technologies developed through the project

The review meetings will be held within 3 months of each anniversary of the project start date. These dates will be set by the APC in consultation with you.

APC will agree the content and structure of the review with you in advance, and in consultation with Innovate UK.
